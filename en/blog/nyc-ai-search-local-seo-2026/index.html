Local SEO for NYC Businesses in the AI Search Era

How ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews cite local businesses — 2026 local SEO + GEO for NYC Chinese businesses: schema, llms.txt, GBP, content clusters.

Customers increasingly ask ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews for "best Flushing contractor" or "Manhattan law firm" — not only blue links. AI still relies on crawlable, structured, consistent websites and Google Business Profile. Local SEO isn't dead; it needs GEO (Generative Engine Optimization).

How AI search "learns" your business

Models synthesize: service pages, About/Contact, blog depth, Maps reviews, third-party listings when NAP matches.

Thin homepages with no service URLs and mismatched GBP data get skipped — competitors with clearer entity signals win citations.

2026 basics unchanged

Local SEO core: GBP, mobile UX, Core Web Vitals, borough + industry keywords, real reviews and case studies.

On-site: dedicated URLs per service (e.g. our SEO page), FAQs, BreadcrumbList and LocalBusiness schema. See common website SEO mistakes.

GEO additions for AI entities

llms.txt: root-level entity summary and key URLs (70nyc.com/llms.txt) for AI crawlers.

Structured data: Organization/LocalBusiness with knowsAbout, hasOfferCatalog; Service schema on service pages; FAQPage on area FAQs.

Quotable copy: clear H2/H3 and definitional sentences AI can cite — not core info locked in images or JS-only widgets.

Content & E-E-A-T

"Best NYC agency" fails for humans and AI. Specify boroughs served, industries, timelines, plausible outcome ranges.

Interlink blogs and area pages — e.g. Flushing restaurant Maps SEO → Flushing services.

AI marketing + SEO together

AI chat and lead follow-up (AI marketing) handle conversion; SEO/GEO handle discovery.

Avoid mass AI spam pages — search and AI systems penalize low-quality scale.

Quick checklist

☑ NAP matches GBP ☑ Service pages exist ☑ 2–3 depth articles ☑ schema + sitemap ☑ robots allow AI bots ☑ llms.txt or strong About summary
