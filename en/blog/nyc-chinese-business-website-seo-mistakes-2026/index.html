7 Website SEO Mistakes NYC Chinese Businesses Make

Why your NYC Chinese business website doesn't rank — bilingual NAP issues, migration 404s, keyword stuffing, missing local pages, and fixes.

Many Flushing, Manhattan, and Brooklyn owners invested in a website but still don't show up on Google. The issue is usually structural — and fixable. Here are seven mistakes we see most often after 13 years serving Chinese-owned businesses in New York.

Mistake 1: Inconsistent bilingual NAP data

Different phone numbers on your site, Google Maps, and Yelp confuse Google about whether you're one business. Mixed Chinese/English address formats make it worse.

Fix: Unify name, address, and phone everywhere — especially your contact page and Google Business Profile.

Mistake 2: Targeting only broad keywords

"NYC web design" is extremely competitive. Contractors, restaurants, and law firms should prioritize neighborhood + service long-tail terms first.

Fix: Build dedicated area landing pages — see our service areas structure instead of keyword-stuffing the homepage footer.

Mistake 3: No 301 redirects after migration

Switching from WordPress or HostGator without redirects leaves 404s and splits index equity — a common 2025–2026 Search Console pattern.

Fix: 301 old URLs to new service or blog pages; resubmit sitemap.xml.

Mistake 4: Image-only menus and portfolios

JPEG menus and before/after galleries with no text give Google nothing to index for "kitchen remodeling Flushing" or similar queries.

Fix: Add descriptive copy and alt text for every service and dish.

Mistake 5: Split blog subdomain

Hosting blog.70nyc.com separately from your main domain treats content as two sites — blog authority won't pass to your primary domain.

Fix: Consolidate on 70nyc.com/blog/ with 301 redirects from the old subdomain.

Mistake 6: Ignoring Google Maps and reviews

Perfect on-page SEO still won't crack the Local Pack with an unclaimed GBP and zero reviews.

Fix: Claim GBP, post weekly, collect reviews — see our Flushing Google Maps guide.

Mistake 7: Launch and forget

Sites unchanged for years with no internal links to services or blog posts signal inactivity to Google.

Fix: Interlink home → services → areas → blog; update quarterly. Need an audit? See NYC SEO services.
